Vader is the On-Grid's chief space commander and the single most dangerous body the network fields off-planet. He is not the C1 villain and he is not the C2 saviour; he is the third blade on the table, whose presence makes both of the first two reconsider their positions. Every asteroid the [Asteroid Mining](/codex/asteroid-mining) programme has pulled in, every off-world node the network runs, sits inside territory he has either cleared or walked through.

## The suit

The suit is the character. It is a full-body system — armour, biology, signal, power — built to carry a human body that has been pushed far past what a body is supposed to carry. Inside the suit are stacked [e-hair](/codex/e-hair) arrays, bacterium-derived parasites turned tool, and two further power sources the archive has never confirmed the origin of. The suit is the reason he is alive. The suit is also the reason he is constrained: without the modifications he would be a different kind of fighter, freer, and likely a shorter-lived one. He accepts the trade.

## What he knows

He knows more than the earth-side cast. Years in space, at range, against the [Alien Empire](/codex/alien-empire)'s outer layer, have given him ground-truth the On-Grid otherwise has to infer. He shares almost none of it. The small circle he reports to includes one of the founders of the Grid Network and a single survivor of the prequel arc. Outside that circle, what he knows is operational fog.

## The record

He has, while serving the Grid utopia, decimated populations on off-world rocks. The On-Grid caste that approves his operations knows this. Most of the On-Grid civilian population does not. He is not secretly a villain and he is not openly a hero; he is the instrument the network uses when the alternative is the network losing a node it cannot afford to lose. That calculation is the one he has signed his life over to.

## The twist

The prequel arc, when it arrives, is set up to show a younger man — same body, no suit, different decisions — who becomes the person in the suit. The story's weight rests on what that younger man chose and what he failed to refuse. The suit is not the transformation. The suit is the record of it.

## Why he matters

[Illum](/codex/illum) is the mind the counter-offensive uses to read the enemy. Vader is the hand the network uses to reach targets the counter-offensive cannot. When the Alien Empire's elite force lands in full — not the small family unit already on earth — Vader is the piece the On-Grid reaches for first. Every other piece is a second-choice.
